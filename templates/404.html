Hunt–Lenox Globe

The Hunt–Lenox Globe or Lenox Globe, dating from ca. 1510,[1][2] is the second- or third-oldest known terrestrial globe, after the Erdapfel of 1492. It is housed by the Rare Book Division of the New York Public Library.

It is notable as one of only two known instances of a historical map actually using the phrase HC SVNT DRACONES (in Latin hic sunt dracones means "here are dragons".)
